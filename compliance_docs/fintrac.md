# FINTRAC - Proceeds of Crime (Money Laundering) and Terrorist Financing Regulations

## Key Obligations

- **Customer Identification and Verification (KYC)**: Reporting entities must verify the identity of clients using prescribed methods before providing certain services.
- **Record Keeping**: Maintain records of transactions, client information, and due diligence for a minimum of **7 years**.
- **Suspicious Transaction Reporting (STR)**: Must report any financial transaction where there are reasonable grounds to suspect it relates to money laundering or terrorist financing.
- **Terrorist Property Reporting**: Report possession or control of property owned or controlled by a terrorist or terrorist group.
- **Beneficial Ownership Identification**: Take reasonable measures to identify beneficial owners.
- **No Tipping Off**: Prohibited from informing clients that a report has been made to FINTRAC.
- **Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Program**: Must implement a compliance program including policies, procedures, risk assessment, training, and ongoing monitoring.

**Important**: These obligations apply to financial entities, money services businesses, and other reporting entities in Canada.
