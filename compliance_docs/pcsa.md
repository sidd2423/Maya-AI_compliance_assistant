# PCSA - Payment Clearing and Settlement Act

## Key Objectives

- **Systemic Risk Management**: Reduce systemic risk in clearing and settlement systems that could affect the stability of the Canadian financial system.
- **Oversight by Bank of Canada**: The Bank has authority to designate, oversee, and issue directives to clearing and settlement systems.
- **Settlement Finality**: Provides legal certainty for settlement rules, netting, and finality of payments.
- **Risk Control**: Systems must be properly designed and operated to control payments system risk and systemic risk.
- **Designation Criteria**: Systems can be designated if they pose systemic risk or payments system risk.

## Important Principles

- Protection of the overall stability and efficiency of the Canadian financial system.
- Legal framework for netting, settlement finality, and collateral enforcement.
- Bank of Canada powers include issuing directives, providing liquidity, and acting as central counterparty when necessary.

**Scope**: Applies to designated clearing and settlement systems involving Canadian dollar payments.
